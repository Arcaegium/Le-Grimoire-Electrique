Pax Aetherium, the Opal Standard

Construction, Appearance, and Value

Pax Aetherium is the lifeblood of the economy and energy infrastructure in Cirque Electrique. These coins are produced by Aetherial Engines, devices that absorb ambient Phantasmal energies, distill and purify them, and solidify the refined energy into coin-shaped ingots. Each type of Pax Aetherium reflects a different level of refinement, with higher-grade engines producing more valuable coins. The decentralized production of these coins, along with their direct utility as an energy source to power Wards and other Electrique Devices, has made Pax Aetherium the primary currency throughout all of Humanity. Below is a detailed description of the four types of Pax Aetherium. The Opal Aetherium is considered the standard, and the other Pax Aetherium are measured against it for comparative value.

1. Copper Aetherium (Value: 0.01 Opals)

Construction: Produced by Crude Aetherial Engines, Copper Aetherium coins are the most basic form of this currency. They are created from the rawest, least refined etheric energies, distilled just enough to form a solid, stable coin.

Appearance: These coins are a dull, reddish-brown color, with visible imperfections and rough edges. The surface is marked by a simple, geometric design etched into the metal, representing the basic industrial processes involved in their creation. Despite their crude appearance, the coins emit a faint, greenish glow, a sign of the etheric energy they contain.

Value: Copper Aetherium coins are the smallest denomination, valued at 0.01 Opals. They are commonly used for everyday transactions among the working class and are often the most abundant form of Aetherium in circulation.

2. Cobalt Aetherium Coin (Value: 0.10 Opals)

Construction: Cobalt Aetherium coins are created by Distilling Aetherial Engines, which are more refined and efficient than their Crude counterparts. These engines produce a higher grade of Ether, resulting in coins of greater value and stability.

Appearance: Cobalt coins are a deep, metallic blue with a polished, reflective surface. The intricate designs on these coins depict flowing currents of energy, symbolizing the more advanced distillation process that creates them. Each coin emits a soft, blue glow, indicative of its higher etheric charge.

Value: These coins are valued at 0.10 Opals, making them a more significant unit of currency. Cobalt Aetherium coins are typically used for larger transactions and are favored by the middle class.

3. Opal Aetherium Coin (Value: 1.00 Opals)

Construction: Produced by Refined Aetherial Engines, Opal Aetherium coins are the standard unit of currency. These engines operate with high precision, purifying etheric energies to create coins of exceptional clarity and power.

Appearance: Opal coins are semi-translucent, with a soft, iridescent glow that shifts through a spectrum of colors as they catch the light. The surface of the coin is smooth and cool to the touch, with intricate patterns of celestial motifs engraved into the metal. The central core of each coin shines with a bright, opalescent light, symbolizing its purity and stability.

Value: Opal Aetherium coins are the baseline currency, valued at 1.00 Opals. They are widely accepted across all social classes, but are most typically found amongst the Upper class.

4. Gold Aetherium Coin (Value: 10.00 Opals)

  • Construction: Gold Aetherium coins are the product of Pure Aetherial Engines, the most advanced and sophisticated of all Aetherial technologies. These engines operate with unparalleled efficiency, distilling the purest form of etheric energy into coins of the highest value and quality. The production process is intricate, involving multiple stages of filtration and refinement to ensure the utmost purity.

  • Appearance: Gold Aetherium coins are resplendent, with a radiant, golden hue that exudes an aura of luxury and power. The surface of each coin is flawlessly smooth, with delicate, filigree patterns that shimmer with an inner light. The edges are finely ridged, and the central emblem is a large, multi-faceted gemstone that glows with a steady, golden light. These coins are both a symbol of wealth and a powerful source of energy, their brilliance reflecting the high status of their owners.

  • Value: Valued at 10.00 Opals, Gold Aetherium coins are the most valuable and rare form of currency. They are typically used in large transactions and are a common reserve of wealth among the upper class and elite. Due to their significant value, these coins are often used in investments, major business deals, and other high-stakes financial endeavors.

Cost of Living: Economic Structure and Class Dynamics

The society of Cirque Electrique is deeply stratified, with a rigid hierarchy that determines access to resources, technology, and basic necessities. The wealthy elite enjoy a life of luxury, insulated from the harsh realities of the world by their wealth and control over Wards and Ether resources. The middle class, while more comfortable, still faces the constant pressure to maintain their status, balancing their income with the costs of keeping up appearances. The working class, forming the backbone of the economy, labors long hours in dangerous conditions, earning just enough to survive and contribute to daily Ward fees that protect them from Phantom attacks. The poor, on the other hand, live a precarious existence, relying on communal efforts to maintain Wards and survive day-to-day.

Living Conditions and Standards

The cost of living varies significantly across the social strata. For the working class, expenses such as housing, food, utilities, and contributions to neighborhood Wards are essential but burdensome. Electrique devices like the EtherGlow Orbs and AetherLift Apparatus are limited luxuries, used sparingly to conserve Pax Aetherium. Middle-class families, while more comfortable, still prioritize spending on maintaining their status, with greater access to Electrique devices and better living conditions. The elite, by contrast, have access to the finest technologies and enjoy a life of excess, with their cost of living reflecting the high price of luxury and security.

Social Expectations and Class Behavior

Social norms and expectations heavily influence spending patterns. The middle class is acutely aware of the need to present themselves well, often spending a significant portion of their income on clothing, personal Wards, and other status symbols. The working class, while focused on survival, also faces social pressures to contribute to communal Wards and maintain a basic standard of living. The poor, with little to no disposable income, often operate in informal economies, bartering and relying on community support to meet their needs. The disparity in income and cost of living across the classes creates a clear delineation between those who can afford to enjoy the benefits of technological progress and those who struggle to survive.

Technological Impact and Accessibility

Electrique devices play a crucial role in everyday life, but their accessibility varies greatly by class. The elite have the means to own and operate the most advanced technologies, while the middle class can afford a moderate range of devices, balancing utility and cost. The working class, however, must carefully ration their use of Electrique devices, prioritizing essential functions like lighting and transportation while forgoing more specialized or luxury items. The poor, largely excluded from the benefits of these technologies, rely on rudimentary tools and communal resources to get by.

Regional and Urban Variations

The distribution of resources and technology also varies by region and city-state. Wealthier cities with stronger economies and more robust Wards can offer better living conditions and access to technology for their residents, particularly the middle and upper classes. In contrast, poorer or more isolated areas may struggle with limited access to Electrique devices, higher costs of living, and greater vulnerability to Phantom attacks. The economic and social divide is not just vertical (between classes) but also horizontal, with regional disparities further complicating the landscape of Cirque Electrique.
