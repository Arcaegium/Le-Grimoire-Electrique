In the world of Cirque Electrique, performers incorporate steampunk and electropunk technologies to enhance their acts and combat the spreading malevolence. Here are some examples of how each archetype might appear and utilize these innovations:

1. Strongman

  • Description: The Strongman of Cirque Electrique is an imposing figure clad in a robust, steam-powered exoskeleton that amplifies his natural strength. This exoskeleton is adorned with brass and copper plates, featuring intricate gearwork and hydraulic pistons that enable him to lift and manipulate enormous weights effortlessly. In his performances, he showcases feats of strength, such as bending iron bars and lifting massive weights with ease. Against evil forces, his exoskeleton serves as a formidable defense, allowing him to engage in close combat with corrupted entities, using its reinforced plating to withstand attacks and its powerful hydraulics to deliver devastating blows.

  • Visual Details: The Strongman’s imposing presence is highlighted by the detailed steam-powered exoskeleton. Brass and copper plates, intricate gearwork, and hydraulic pistons are prominently featured.

  • Atmosphere: The sound of hissing steam and the clank of metal creates an immersive environment. His ability to effortlessly lift massive weights or withstand blows adds a sense of awe and power.

  • Powers:

    • Boost/Lower Trait (*): Using the exoskeleton’s enhancers to boost Strength, Agility, or Vigor for superhuman feats of strength.

    • Protection (*): Activating reinforced plating as protective armor (e.g. a burst of steam-hardening or energy shield) to absorb damage.

    • Smite (*): Channeling the suit’s power into his fists or a heavy prop, increasing melee damage (fluff as hydraulics delivering extra force or electrifying a punch).

    • Havoc (+): Stomping the ground or clanging the exoskeleton to produce a shockwave that knocks back or disrupts enemies (fits the idea of overwhelming strength).

    • Deflection (+): Using mechanical reflexes or magnetic fields in the suit to deflect attacks away (thematically a steam-driven block or gadget that causes bullets to veer off).

2. Flameblower

  • Description: The Flameblower performer wields a steam-powered apparatus designed to project intense bursts of flame. His suit is fitted with a custom-built flamethrower that channels a blend of pressurized steam and incendiary compounds, creating controlled but impressive fire effects. In his act, he performs daring fire-breathing routines and intricate pyrotechnic displays. In battle, his Flameblower becomes a potent weapon against dark entities, using its fiery arsenal to scorch corrupted machinery and fend off malevolent creatures, effectively wielding fire as both a tool of spectacle and a weapon of defense.

  • Visual Details: The steam-powered Flameblower is highlighted by its brass fittings and the burst of intense, controlled flames. The performer’s suit gleams with the heat of the fire.

  • Atmosphere: The roaring fire, the smell of burning fuel, and the scorching heat add both drama and danger to his performance or combat scene.

  • Powers:

    • Burst (*): Unleashing a cone of flame from the custom flamethrower – perfect for short-range gouts of fire hitting multiple targets (the classic flamethrower effect).

    • Blast (*): Shooting an explosive fireball or pressurized flame bomb that affects a Medium Burst Template – fits his pyrotechnic displays turned offensive.

    • Bolt (*): Firing a focused jet of flame at a single target (a stream of fire or incendiary projectile). This is a simple and reliable ranged attack.

    • Light/Darkness (+): Using his gadgetry to illuminate areas or snuff out lights. For example, he could ignite something to light the scene or create black smoke to obscure (darkness) as part of his show.

    • Elemental Manipulation (+): Minor pyrokinetic tricks via tech, like igniting small fires, heating objects, or creating puffs of smoke for show. Not powerful in combat, but very flavorful for a fire-breather.

3. Escape Artist (Master of Chains)

  • Description: The Escape Artist, a master of chains and locks, employs a range of clockwork gadgets to enhance her escape routines. Her costume includes integrated, tiny mechanical devices that can unlock or manipulate complex locks with precise movements. In her performances, she is often seen emerging from intricate, self-designed contraptions or elaborate traps. When confronting dark forces, her escape skills are augmented with electrique advancements, such as electrically powered lock-picking tools and quick-release mechanisms, and electrified chain whips, all allowing her to escape and outmaneuver enemies with ease, slipping through their grasp and using her agility to strike from unexpected angles.

  • Visual Details: Focus on the clockwork gadgets integrated into the Escape Artist’s costume and the electrified chain whips. Her agile movements are emphasized along with the mechanical efficiency of her tools.

  • Atmosphere: The sound of clicking locks and the crackle of electricity in the chains enhances the tension and excitement of her escapes and confrontations.

  • Powers:

    • Entangle (*): Tossing out electrified chains or padlocks to bind enemies in place. Mechanically, this fits perfectly (target is Entangled or Bound) and matches her use of chains to restrain foes.

    • Deflection (*): Her uncanny escape training makes her hard to hit – flavored as graceful dodges or using a whirling chain as a parrying tool. This power gives attackers a –2 (or –4) to hit her, which is both thematically and mechanically strong for an evasive character.

    • Stun (*): Emitting a shocking surge through her chains to jolt a target’s nervous system. This can momentarily incapacitate an enemy (Stun power forces a Vigor roll or they become Stunned) – very useful when slipping free or taking someone out silently.

    • Teleport (+): A flavorful escape trick – a concealed device that lets her vanish from bonds or traps in a flash of electricity. Short-range teleportation could simulate her greatest escapes (though as a Seasoned power, it’s a more advanced trick).

    • Boost/Lower Trait (+): Enhancing her Agility or Thievery skill with adrenaline or gadgetry to pick locks and escape even faster, or lowering an enemy’s Agility (e.g. making their limbs go numb with an electrical shock) to slip from their grasp.

4. Knife Thrower

  • Description: The Knife Thrower employs a set of precision-engineered throwing knives, each designed with sharp, streamlined edges and embedded with mechanical enhancements for accuracy and stability. His act involves throwing these knives with deadly precision at spinning targets or even his assistant, who is securely bound in place. In battle, the knives can be electrified, poisoned, or even affixed with small explosives to deliver a variety of effects to enemies or disrupt corrupted machinery, making them a versatile tool for both performance and combat. The precision of his throws ensures that he can strike at critical points and neutralize threats effectively.

  • Visual Details: Highlight the precision-engineered knives, their sharpness, and mechanical enhancements. Describe the exactness of his throws and the electrifying effects of the knives.

  • Atmosphere: The sound of knives slicing through the air and the sparks from electrified blades create a tense and thrilling atmosphere, emphasizing the skill and danger of the act.

  • Powers:

    • Bolt (*): Each thrown knife can be treated as a ranged attack power. With Weird Science, he might have a gadget that propels or energizes his knives, effectively making them Bolts (e.g. an electrified knife or one that injects poison on hit). This is a staple offensive power for single targets.

    • Blast (*): Using knives rigged with small explosives – throw one that detonates on impact, affecting an area (Medium Blast Template). This matches his ability to disrupt groups or machinery with explosive knives and is very potent mechanically.

    • Smite (*): Applying a chemical or electrical charge to his blades to increase their damage before throwing. Smite can add +2 damage (+4 on a raise) to his knives – mechanically strong, especially for a high-precision attacker ensuring each hit counts.

    • Stun (+): Coating a knife with a paralytic toxin or taser charge to shock a target into a Stunned state. This is a flavorful special attack for incapacitating a foe without killing, using the Stun power’s effects.

    • Blind (+): Flicking a blade that emits a flash or powder on impact, obscuring a target’s vision (the Blind power inflicts vision penalties). As a trick shot, this fits the idea of a special trick knife and can set up an enemy for his next attack.

    • Entangle (+): Throwing a knife attached to a cable or bola, snagging an enemy to restrict movement. Not a typical use of knives, but with a little gadget creativity, he could have a trick blade that expands into a snare (mimicking the Entangle power).

5. Clown

  • Description: The Clown blends traditional humor with whimsical custom-made gadgets. His costume is a patchwork of bright electric colors and mechanical elements, including a spring-loaded hat and automated shoes that produce amusing sounds and movements. His acts include slapstick comedy and absurd antics enhanced by these devices. In the fight against evil, his gadgets become more than just props—they can include defensive mechanisms like smoke screens or non-lethal, electrified weapons disguised as part of his comedic routine, providing both distraction and tactical advantage during skirmishes with dark forces. At the same time, the Clown’s understanding of how to draw attention, roll with a hit, and take a fall all make him a valuable front-line combatant.

  • Visual Details: Detail the Clown’s vibrant, mechanical costume with whimsical gadgets. The spring-loaded hat, automated shoes, and other amusing devices are highlighted.

  • Atmosphere: Use comedic and surprising elements like unexpected smoke screens or electrified props to create an entertaining yet strategic combat scene. The Clown’s humor and gadgets add a unique flair.

  • Powers:

    • Deflection (*): The Clown’s unpredictable movements and clever gadgets make him hard to hit. Think of a spring-loaded jack-in-the-box that pops up to intercept an attack, or the Clown simply pratfalling out of harm’s way (–2 to enemy attack rolls is hugely helpful).

    • Confusion (*): Through slapstick antics or a barrage of absurd tricks, the Clown can befuddle opponents, making them Distracted and Vulnerable (the Confusion power). Mechanically, that sets enemies up for defeat, and in-character it’s his specialty to confuse and divert attention.

    • Stun (*): A joy-buzzer handshake or a shocking squirting flower delivers a jolt that stuns a target. This power is mechanically strong (forcing a vigor roll or be Stunned) and fits the Clown’s electrified props used in combat.

    • Obscure (Light/Darkness) (+): Using a smoke bomb or gag umbrella to create a smokescreen (the darkness effect of Light/Darkness). This is mostly for flavor and tactical retreats, concealing the Clown or allies in a pinch.

    • Smite (+): Humorously “enchanting” a slapstick weapon – for instance, overcharging his rubber chicken or mallet so it hits extra hard. The Clown might not seem like a damage-dealer, but a Smite (+2 damage) on his pie-launcher or boxing-glove gun can give his strikes unexpected punch.

    • Relief (+): Comic relief can be literal – using jokes or a comforting gag to rally an ally. The Relief power removes Shaken or Fatigue. It’s a flavorful support move (perhaps patting an ally with a comically oversized hand to snap them out of a daze).

6. Stilt Boxer

  • Description: The Stilt Boxer performs on towering, steam-powered stilts, with boxing gloves on telescoping mechanical fists that give him impressive height and reach advantages, as well as excellent mobility. His stilts are equipped with hydraulic joints that allow for powerful leaps and swift movements. In his act, he demonstrates agility and strength, delivering high-flying kicks and punches while balancing on these mechanical platforms. In combat, the stilts enhance his ability to deliver crushing blows from above, navigate over obstacles, and avoid ground-based attacks, making him a formidable opponent for dark entities that seek to bring chaos.

  • Visual Details: Describe the towering, steam-powered stilts with hydraulic joints in action. The height advantage and powerful kicks are vividly portrayed.

  • Atmosphere: The clanking of the stilts and the sight of high-flying attacks create a dramatic and dynamic scene, enhancing his formidable presence and agility

  • Powers:

    • Boost/Lower Trait (*): Cranking up the stilt hydraulics to boost his Strength or Agility. A boost to Strength makes his punches hit harder; boosting Agility improves balance and Fighting skill on those tall stilts. (Mechanically very useful for a melee fighter.)

    • Sloth/Speed (*): Engaging a piston boost to increase his speed in combat. The Speed effect lets him cover ground or dodge quickly (and with the “Quickness” modifier, could even reduce multi-action penalties – perfect for throwing rapid punches or moving and striking).

    • Smite (*): Powering up his mechanical boxing gloves with a burst of steam or electricity to increase damage. Smite will make his next punch far more devastating – great for overcoming tough foes.

    • Deflection (+): Bobbing and weaving on stilts or using his height advantage to evade attacks. This can be visualized as him leaning out of reach or his stilts pivoting at the last second, making opponents miss (Deflection’s defensive boon).

    • Stun (+): Coming down from above with a crushing blow that dazes the target. This isn’t a separate gadget power per se, but you could treat a particularly thunderous strike (like a spring-loaded downward punch) as the Stun power to knock an enemy senseless.

7. Ventriloquist

  • Description: The Ventriloquist uses advanced mechanical puppets to perform acts of voice mimicry and interactive dialogue. His puppets are sophisticated creations with intricate clockwork mechanisms, some even powered by electricity, allowing them to move with lifelike precision. During performances, the puppets engage in elaborate skits and conversations. Against evil forces, the ventriloquist’s puppets can be equipped with hidden compartments for storing weapons or gadgets, and their mechanical nature allows for discreet surveillance or delivery of messages, helping to gather intelligence on dark forces or distract and disorient enemies during confrontations.

  • Visual Details: Focus on the sophisticated mechanical puppets, their intricate clockwork mechanisms, and how they interact with the Ventriloquist. Detail their lifelike movements and hidden compartments.

  • Atmosphere: The puppets’ movements and dialogue add a layer of intrigue and surprise, with the potential for espionage or distraction enhancing the drama of the scene.

  • Note: This type of performer has taken on the title of “Ventriloquist” because “Puppeteer” was already being commonly used to refer to a type of Phantom.

  • Powers:

    • Puppet (*): The Puppeteer can attempt to take control of any machine that is hosting Aetheric or Phantasmal energy! With an eerie gadget or hypnotic voice, the Ventriloquist can impose his will (the Puppet power lets him control a target’s actions on a failed Spirit roll- in this case the target number will be defined by the amount and purity of energy already in the machine). Mechanically potent, this makes the Puppeteer invaluable when dealing with phantom-infested machinery.

    • Summon Ally (*): Animating one of his mechanical dolls as a fighting ally. For example, he flips a switch and a puppet springs to life to aid the team (use Summon Ally to bring a construct or animated marionette into play). This is very thematic (his puppets aren’t just props, they can literally join the fray) and mechanically gives the party an extra ally.

    • Sound/Silence (+): Projecting his voice or sounds through his puppet to mimic voices or create distractions, as well as damping sound. He could throw his voice to make it seem like there are allies in another direction (Sound), or hush the gears of his puppet when sneaking (Silence). This power is flavorful for a ventriloquist illusionist role.

    • Confusion (+): Using multiple puppets or an especially baffling routine to confound enemies, making them unsure who to target. Perhaps the puppets perform a disorienting skit or sudden loud outburst – the Confusion power then leaves foes Distracted and Vulnerable.

    • Empathy (+): Through the puppet’s engaging dialogue or intimidating persona, the Ventriloquist influences his target’s emotions – mechanically, Empathy can give him +2 on social attacks (after an opposed Spirit roll). This fits his ability to play mind games with a talking puppet (whether soothing or unsettling).

8. Acrobat

  • Description: The Acrobat in Cirque Electrique utilizes a combination of jetpacks, mechanical wings, and advanced harnesses to perform gravity-defying stunts. Her costume includes steam-powered apparatuses that allow for bursts of flight and extraordinary aerial maneuvers and flips. In her performance, she soars through the air with breathtaking agility and grace, deploying colored smoke bombs and fireworks across her path. In the fight against dark forces, the acrobat’s wings keep her above the fray and her fireworks are replaced with grenades. Her expertise with ranged weaponry makes her a deadly threat, enabling her to engage in aerial combat and avoid ground-based attacks with ease.

  • Visual Details: Illustrate the jetpacks and mechanical wings in action, with bursts of flight and graceful aerial maneuvers. The acrobat’s costume should reflect advanced technology and agility.

  • Atmosphere: The sight of her soaring through the air with fireworks or grenades adds an element of high-flying spectacle and excitement, enhancing the aerial combat scenes.

  • Powers:

    • Fly (*): Her mechanical wings enable true flight. The Fly power (Pace 12″ flight) matches her jetpack bursts and aerial acrobatics. Mechanically, flight is a huge advantage for mobility and fits perfectly with her theme (even though Fly is a Veteran power, it’s the ideal way to represent sustained jet-assisted aerial movement).

    • Blast (*): Replacing showy fireworks with live grenades – she can hurl an explosive at groups of enemies. This is the Blast power, dealing area damage, and is highly effective in combat (imagine her tossing a grenade from above into clustered foes).

    • Deflection (*): Constant motion and aerial agility make her hard to target. She can use Deflection (flavored as swift, erratic flight patterns or a burst of chaff from her devices) to cause ranged attacks to miss her while she’s aloft. This keeps her safe while airborne and fits the high-flying daredevil imagery.

    • Burst (+): For closer-range crowd control, she could deploy a spray of shrapnel or flame from a gadget as she flies by (the Burst power’s cone template could represent a wide dispersal grenade or flamethrower burst fired downward). It’s a bit redundant with Blast, but useful for a different area shape.

    • Blind (+): Utilizing a modified flash-bomb firework to blind opponents (the Blind power). As part of her act she had flash powder for effect; in battle this could be a tactical blinder she drops on enemies to impair their vision while she swoops around.

    • Obscure (Light/Darkness) (+): Popping a smoke grenade midair to create a cloud (using the darkness effect) – a flavorful way to cover allies’ approach or her own escape from a sticky situation.

9. Juggler

  • Description: The Juggler employs a set of sophisticated, mechanized props that incorporate glowing orbs and spinning gears. His act features intricate patterns and sequences of juggling, enhanced by the kinetic energy and visual effects of his props. When combating dark forces, the juggler’s props can be modified into a wide variety of effects, both offensive and defensive, such as energy shields, blinding flashes, fiery explosions, kinetic amplifiers, and electrifying shocks, allowing him to neutralize threats or create diversions while maintaining his impressive juggling routines.

  • Visual Details: Describe the sophisticated, mechanized props, their glowing orbs, and spinning gears. The juggling patterns and visual effects should be vividly portrayed.

  • Atmosphere: The kinetic energy and visual effects of the props create a mesmerizing and dynamic scene, with the Juggler’s ability to adapt his props for combat adding an unpredictable element.

  • Powers:

    • Blast (*): With a flourish, the Juggler can toss a volatile orb that explodes in a fiery blast mid-juggle. This is the Blast power (medium blast area damage) and represents his “fiery explosions” trick – very impactful in combat and well-suited to a performer who handles explosive props.

    • Blind (*): One of his glowing juggling orbs can flare with a blinding flash. As the Blind power, this imposes vision penalties on enemies (great setup for the team). It fits the described bright, mesmerizing visual effects of his act being turned against foes.

    • Protection (*): Spinning a ring of kinetic energy or orbiting gadgets as an energy shield. Using the Protection power, he can increase his (or an ally’s) Armor – the “energy shield” mentioned in his description. Mechanically, that makes him or a friend much harder to wound, and thematically it could be him actively juggling defensive devices to intercept attacks.

    • Boost/Lower Trait (*): Via kinetic amplifiers in his props, he can boost his own effective strength, agility, and vigor – for example, by juggling momentum-enhancing devices around them. Conversely, he might lower an enemy’s Agility by hitting them with a weighty juggling club that throws off their balance. This versatility makes Boost/Lower Trait both a strong buff and debuff in his toolkit.

    • Bolt (+): Hurling a single charged sphere or gear as a precise ranged attack (a Bolt of energy or force). This could be an electrified ball that zaps a target. It’s straightforward and likely something he can do as part of juggling (flinging one of his items with deadly accuracy).

    • Damage Field (+): Keeping a halo of spinning, sparking items around himself as a hazardous aura. This would use the Damage Field power (causing continual damage to anyone adjacent). It’s a flashy defensive move – any foe who comes in close gets whacked or shocked by the orbiting juggled objects.

    • Stun (+): Charging one of his juggling props with kinetic force and bouncing it off an enemy’s head to daze them. This could emulate the Stun power, fitting the idea of using kinetic force in creative ways (perhaps an orb that delivers an electrical shock on impact, stunning the target).

10. Animal Tamer

  • Description: The Animal Tamer works with a collection of steam-powered, mechanically enhanced beasts that have been trained to perform alongside him. His attire includes protective gear and control devices that enable him to manage and direct these creatures with precision. In his act, the animals display remarkable tricks and synchronized routines. In combat, the tamer’s animals are equipped with reinforcing modifications, such as armor and metallic claws, allowing them to defend against and repel malevolent entities with a variety of tactics.

  • Visual Details: Focus on the steam-powered, mechanically enhanced beasts and the tamer’s control devices. The animals’ armor and metallic claws should be highlighted.

  • Atmosphere: The roar of the beasts and the sound of their mechanical enhancements create a powerful and commanding presence, making their performance and combat scenes more intense.

  • Power:

    • Beast Friend (*): The core power for an animal tamer – he can control beasts. Here it’s flavored as using devices and training to direct his mechanical beasts’ actions (rules-wise, Beast Friend lets him command natural animals with simple orders – the GM might allow it for his tech-augmented beasts as well). This is key to making his pets effective in battle.

    • Summon Ally (*): If his creatures are not always by his side, he could summon a mechanical beast when needed. For example, blowing a special whistle to call forth a clockwork lion from the shadows. Summon Ally would conjure an allied creature (the cost based on its Size/abilities). This is mechanically potent, as it brings extra fighters to the field, and fits if he occasionally “unleashes” new animals from cages or hidden compartments.

    • Boost Trait (*): Rousing his animal with a stimulant or tweaking its machinery to boost its Strength or Vigor. For instance, he hits a switch on his bear’s harness to enrage it, making it hit harder. This can also apply to boosting his own Riding or Persuasion when dealing with beasts (to symbolize his exceptional rapport).

    • Fear (+): Letting loose a fearsome roar in unison with a beast or using a gadget to amplify a creature’s snarl, causing enemies to make a Fear check. The Fear power fits the dramatic terror of a roaring cybernetic tiger on the battlefield – a great crowd-control effect, though situational.

    • Healing (+): Tending to his animal companions with quick mechanical repairs or medicinal tonics. Healing can patch up Wounds on his beasts (or allies). It’s more of a support role for the tamer – flavorful as the “veterinarian/inventor” side of him – but can be clutch to keep his favorite creature in the fight.

11. Snake Charmer

  • Description: The Snake Charmer uses a collection of mechanical snakes that are enhanced with electrique technology. These mechanical creatures can perform complex movements and are equipped with various gadgets and weaponry. In his act, the charmer manipulates the snakes in synchronized routines. In combat, the mechanical snakes can be used for reconnaissance, as they can navigate tight spaces, or they can be used to trip or restrict opponents and deliver poison or shocks, along with other offensive capabilities, making them versatile tools for both performance and battle.

  • Visual Details: Describe the mechanical snakes with their electrique enhancements. The synchronized movements and weaponry of the snakes should be detailed.

  • Atmosphere: The mechanical snakes’ movements and the Charmer’s manipulation create a captivating and eerie atmosphere, enhancing the visual and tactical impact of his performance.

  • Powers:

    • Entangle (*): Sending his mechanical snakes to coil around an enemy’s legs or body, immobilizing them. This directly mirrors the Entangle power (binding foes so they can’t move and are easier to hit) and is highly thematic – the image of snakes wrapping up a target is spot on.

    • Stun (*): One of the snakes strikes with an electrifying bite, stunning the target’s nervous system. This uses the Stun power, which forces a vigor roll or leaves the victim Stunned. Mechanically, that’s strong, and it fits the idea of the snakes delivering electrical shocks.

    • Lower Trait (*): A mechanical viper injects venom that weakens its prey – for example, lowering the target’s Vigor or Strength. Lower Trait can temporarily reduce an enemy’s attribute or skill, reflecting the debilitating effect of poison. This can turn the tide by softening up tough enemies (and feels appropriate for venomous snakes).

    • Slumber (+): A specialized toxin from the snake puts the victim to sleep. This would be the Slumber power – instead of magical sandman dust, it’s a sedative venom that knocks people out for an hour or until shaken awake. A flavorful non-lethal option that highlights the charmer’s mastery over venom.

    • Burrow (+): The snakes’ slim design could allow them to slither through tiny cracks or underground. Using Burrow on a snake ally would let it disappear into the earth and pop up elsewhere (useful for infiltration or surprise attacks). It’s a niche power, but it fits the idea of a snake going where others can’t.

    • Summon Ally (+): If the Snake Charmer carries a basket of mechanical serpents, he might “deploy” another snake when needed. Summon Ally can represent releasing an extra snake into the fray (the ally being a small construct). Mechanically, additional allies are useful, though the charmer likely already has his pets without needing to summon.

His costume includes integrated projectors and holographic generators that enable him to conjure realistic, ephemeral images. During his act, he performs feats of illusion that captivate and mystify the audience. In combat, the Illusionist’s devices can create diversions, conceal his allies, or mislead enemies, making him a valuable asset in tactical scenarios. His ability to create convincing illusions can confuse and disorient foes, turning the tide of battle in favor of the circus.

  • Visual Details: Focus on the electrique devices and holographic generators integrated into the Illusionist’s costume. The visual deceptions and illusions should be vividly portrayed.

  • Atmosphere: The creation of illusions and visual effects adds a layer of intrigue and unpredictability, making the Illusionist’s performances and combat scenes both mesmerizing and strategic.

  • Powers:

    • Illusion (*): The signature ability – creating realistic visual (and possibly auditory) illusions to deceive enemies. The Illusion power allows him to produce images that look real. This aligns perfectly with his holographic projectors that conjure “ephemeral images.” Mechanically, it can fool opponents or conceal hazards, very on-theme and useful.

    • Invisibility (*): Bending light around herself or an ally to become unseen. With the Illusionist’s tech, this could be a sort of cloaking field. Invisibility is a powerful Seasoned power, but it fits his role of concealing allies and himself in combat for tactical advantage.

    • Disguise (+): Projecting an illusion over a person to make them look like someone else. The Disguise power is essentially a specialized illusion that sticks to a target. This is great for infiltration or escape, aligning with the Illusionist’s ability to “mislead enemies” and assume different appearances during acts.

    • Confusion (+): Crafting a rapid series of illusory effects or phantom sounds that distract and confuse foes. While Illusion can do a lot narratively, the Confusion power has a straightforward mechanical effect (Distracted + Vulnerable on targets) that could represent an illusionary assault on the senses. It’s a nice flavorful addition to truly befuddle an opponent with overwhelming sensory tricks.

    • Deflection (+): Surrounding himself with mirror images or light-bending fields to throw off enemy aim. As a power, Deflection would imply minor illusionary duplicates or refractions that impose a penalty on attacks against him. It’s thematically appropriate (classic stage magicians seem to be where they’re not) and helps survivability.

13. Trick Shooter

  • Description: The Trick Shooter wields a set of precision-engineered firearms and clockwork weaponry, each designed for accuracy and adaptability. His act includes sharpshooting and elaborate trick shots. In combat, his firearms can be customized for various effects, such as rapid-fire bursts, electrified shots, or specialized ammunition. The Trick Shooter’s skills make him effective at both long-range engagements and close-quarters combat, using his weaponry to strike down dark entities with pinpoint precision.

  • Visual Details: Highlight the precision-engineered firearms and clockwork weaponry, including their adaptability and effects. The Trick Shooter’s sharpshooting skills and customizations should be detailed.

  • Atmosphere: The sound of firearms and the precision of trick shots create a thrilling and intense atmosphere, enhancing the Trick Shooter’s role in both performance and combat.

  • Powers:

    • Bolt (*): For precision shots with special effects – e.g. a charged bullet or lightning round. Bolt is a go-to ranged attack power. The Trick Shooter could have a custom pistol that fires energy bolts or carries different ammo types (shock, incendiary, etc.), making this more than just a regular Shooting roll. It’s mechanically efficient and versatile (can even fire multiple bolts at extra cost for a “fan the hammer” feel).

    • Blast (*): Loading an explosive cartridge or grenade shell to hit multiple targets. This mimics the Blast power, letting him do area damage (maybe a rifle grenade or a trick shot that ricochets into a group and blows up). Mechanically, this gives him crowd-control capability that a normal shooter wouldn’t have without explosives.

    • Boost/Lower Trait (*): Tinkering with a scope or using a performance enhancer to boost his Shooting trait for a short time. With Boost Trait (Shooting), the Trick Shooter can dramatically improve his accuracy (raise his die type, which is huge for making those impossible shots). This could be a stimulant injection or a clockwork targeting mechanism – mechanically optimal for trick shots and called shots.

    • Burst (+): Unleashing a spray of bullets in a wide fan. The Burst power (a cone attack for 2d6 damage) can represent him using a trick like fanning a six-gun or a shotgun blast. It’s a bit redundant since he likely can just do automatic fire with a normal gun, but as a Weird Science power it could allow a controlled cone of effect even with a weapon that normally doesn’t do that.

    • Smite (+): Temporarily enhancing his firearm’s rounds for extra punch. For example, activating a dial on his rifle to give all shots enhanced damage. Smite isn’t usually needed for a gunslinger (since they can just shoot more), but if expecting a high-toughness foe, this ensures each hit counts harder. A raise can be decisive against armored targets.

    • Farsight (+): Utilizing a high-tech scope or goggles to see distant targets clearly. Farsight reduces Range penalties by enhancing vision. This power is great for a sniper-type Trick Shooter, letting him hit targets at extreme range with ease – a mechanical edge in ranged combat and a logical gadget for a trick shot expert.

    • Entangle/Blind/Stun (+): As additional trick shots, he could have net bullets, flashbang rounds, or taser shots. For instance, a net round that Entangles a foe, a dazzling flare round to Blind them, or a taser bullet that Stuns on impact. These are flavorful options that give him non-lethal or control tools beyond just dealing damage. They might see less use (hence marked flavor), but they fit the concept of having specialized ammunition for any scenario.
