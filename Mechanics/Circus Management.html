Circus Management Rules – Cirque Electrique

In Cirque Electrique, players do not control individual characters. Instead, each player manages a small troupe of performers. The troupe begins with one performer per player and may undertake one Performance per day, with only one Act per Performance at the start.

Players rotate which performers they play during missions or shows. Tasks are assigned each in-game day. Missions grant advancements to individual performers, while Performances increase the troupe’s Reputation, unlocking new capabilities. Chores sustain circus function and prevent penalties.


Daily Assignment Loop

Each in-game day, every performer must be assigned to one of the following tasks:

Missions: Performer gains 1 Advancement if successful.

Performance: Earn Reputation based on outcome.

Chores: Maintain circus operations. Prevent penalties.

A performer can only do one task per day. Those assigned to chores cannot perform or go on missions.


Missions

Each successful mission grants 1 Advancement to each participating performer.

Advancements follow standard Savage Worlds rules.

No Reputation is required to advance via missions.


Performance Mechanics Overview

Act Structure

Each Performance is composed of one or more Acts.

Each Act includes:

1 Lead Performer

Any number of Supporting Performers (using Savage Worlds Support rules)

Support Mechanics

Each success or raise on a Support roll adds +1 to the Lead Performer’s roll (maximum of +4).

If the Act theme matches an audience preference for the location, the Lead Performer gains an additional +2 bonus.

Total modifier for Lead = up to +6.


Performance Resolution Table

Result Reputation Effect
Critical Failure –2 Rep (crowd backlash, humiliating failure)
Failure 0 Rep
Success +1 Rep
Raise(s) +1 Rep per Raise
  • Reputation from all Acts is totaled to determine the raw show result.


Zone-Based Reputation Scaling

After totaling raw reputation, apply a multiplier based on the performance zone:

Zone Entry Requirement Multiplier Notes
Poor None ×0.5 Open access, minimal gain
Working 20 Reputation ×1.0 Standard venues, no restrictions
Middle 60 Reputation ×1.5 Higher expectations, prestige crowd
Upper 100 Reputation + Pay 10 Rep ×2.0 Elite class, must pay to access
  • Upper Zone Tax: Players must spend 10 Rep prior to performing.
  • The multiplier is applied after raw reputation is calculated.

Morale & Availability

Performers left idle for 2 days become Demoralized: –1 to all Trait rolls.

Penalty is removed after the performer works for a day.

If Demoralized for 5 days, the performer may leave the troupe unless rallied by a successful social Test or reassigned.

These mechanics ensure player decisions matter daily while rewarding both tactical optimization and narrative engagement.
